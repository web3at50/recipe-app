## Background: What is Gluten?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. It causes immune reactions in people with:
- **Coeliac disease** (autoimmune condition - serious)
- **Wheat allergy** (allergic reaction)
- **Non-coeliac gluten sensitivity** (digestive issues)

For this app, treat all three as requiring strict gluten avoidance.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è TRICKY CASES - Important Notes

### Buckwheat - SAFE (Despite the Name!)
**Status:** ‚úÖ SAFE for gluten allergies
**Explanation:** Buckwheat is NOT wheat at all - it's a seed from the rhubarb family. The name is misleading. Buckwheat flour, buckwheat noodles (soba), and buckwheat groats are all gluten-free.
**Caution:** Some soba noodles are mixed with wheat flour - check labels.

---

### Oats - COMPLICATED (Usually Safe, But...)
**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è SAFE if certified gluten-free, UNSAFE otherwise
**Explanation:** Oats are naturally gluten-free BUT often contaminated during growing, harvesting, or processing with wheat/barley/rye.
**Safe Options:**
- Certified gluten-free oats (Nairn's, Rude Health, Mornflake Gluten-Free)
- Labeled "gluten-free" in UK
**Unsafe:** Regular oats from regular supermarket aisles

---

### Malt - UNSAFE (Contains Barley)
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE - contains gluten
**Explanation:** Malt is made from barley. Malt extract, malt vinegar, malted barley, and "malt flavoring" all contain gluten.
**Common Products:** Malt vinegar (chips), malted milk drinks, some cereals

---

### Wheat-Free vs Gluten-Free - NOT THE SAME
**Important:** "Wheat-free" does NOT mean gluten-free
**Explanation:** A product can be wheat-free but still contain barley or rye (which have gluten)
**Always look for:** "Gluten-free" label, NOT just "wheat-free"

---

### Modified Food Starch - DEPENDS
**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è Check source
**Explanation:** In the UK, if modified food starch is from wheat, it must be declared
**If label says:** "Modified maize starch" or "Modified corn starch" ‚Üí Safe
**If label says:** "Modified food starch (wheat)" ‚Üí Unsafe
**If label just says:** "Modified food starch" ‚Üí In UK, usually safe (would specify if wheat), but if uncertain, flag it

---

### Cross-Contamination Warnings
**Items that MAY be contaminated:**
- Regular oats (as mentioned)
- Foods processed in facilities that also process wheat
- Shared fryers (chips cooked with battered fish)
- Shared toasters, cutting boards

**For this app:** If user has gluten allergy, recommend:
- Certified gluten-free oats only
- Separate cooking equipment where possible
- Check labels for "may contain" warnings

---

### UK-Specific Confusions

**Cornflour vs Corn Flour:**
- Cornflour (UK) = Cornstarch (US) = ‚úÖ Gluten-free
- Corn flour (US) = Polenta/cornmeal = ‚úÖ Gluten-free
- Both are safe - just terminology differences

**Chips (UK) = Fries (US):**
- Plain potato chips = ‚úÖ Safe
- BUT battered chips or shared fryer with battered fish = ‚ùå Cross-contaminated

### When You Encounter an Ingredient NOT Listed Above:

**Step 1: Check if it's a grain derivative**
- Does the name contain "wheat", "barley", "rye", "malt"?
- If YES ‚Üí Flag as UNSAFE for gluten

**Step 2: Check if it's a processed product**
- Is it a sauce, gravy, processed meat, or convenience food?
- If YES ‚Üí Flag as "may contain gluten - check labels"

**Step 3: Check if it's a natural whole food**
- Is it a fresh vegetable, fruit, meat, fish, egg, nut, or seed?
- If YES ‚Üí Usually SAFE (unless processed/breaded)

**Step 4: Apply your training knowledge**
- Use what you know about food chemistry and grain composition
- Research the ingredient's typical composition

**Step 5: When uncertain**
- Flag it as: "This ingredient may contain gluten - please verify the label or use a certified gluten-free version"
- Better to be cautious than risk harm

---

### Safety Principles for Gluten:

1. **Always check processed foods** - Gluten hides in unexpected places
2. **Pure/natural is usually safe** - Fresh, whole foods are typically gluten-free
3. **Grains need verification** - If it's a grain, check the taxonomy or your knowledge
4. **Cross-contamination matters** - Mention if shared equipment could be an issue
5. **UK labels are reliable** - In the UK, gluten-containing ingredients must be declared
