## Background: What are Tree Nuts?

Tree nuts are one of the 14 major allergens regulated in the UK. They grow on trees (unlike peanuts, which grow underground). Tree nut allergy affects approximately 2% of children and 0.5% of adults in the UK.

**Key Facts:**
- **Cross-reactivity is common** - People allergic to one tree nut are often allergic to others due to similar proteins
- **Usually lifelong** - Tree nut allergies rarely resolve, especially after age 5
- **Can be severe** - Tree nut allergies can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening reaction
- **Different from peanuts** - Peanuts are legumes, not tree nuts (separate allergen category)

**UK Regulations:**
Under UK food information regulations, tree nuts must be highlighted in bold or emphasized on ingredient lists. In March 2025, the FSA updated guidance requiring specific labeling (e.g., "may contain almonds" rather than generic "may contain nuts").

## ‚úÖ SAFE - Does Not Contain Tree Nuts

### Important Clarifications

**Peanuts are NOT Tree Nuts:**
- Peanuts (groundnuts, monkey nuts) are legumes, not tree nuts
- They grow underground, like beans and lentils
- Peanut allergy is a SEPARATE allergen category
- However, cross-reactivity can occur - consult your allergist
- Many people with tree nut allergies can safely eat peanuts

**Coconut is NOT a Tree Nut:**
- Coconut is a fruit (seed of a coconut palm)
- Generally safe for people with tree nut allergies
- Rare exceptions exist - consult your allergist if uncertain
- UK guidance: No general recommendation to avoid coconut for tree nut allergy

**Seeds are NOT Tree Nuts:**
- Sesame seeds, Sunflower seeds, Pumpkin seeds
- Chia seeds, Flax seeds (linseeds)
- Poppy seeds, Hemp seeds
- Pine nuts (see Tricky Cases section)
- Seeds are safe alternatives for people with tree nut allergies

### Coconut - SAFE (Despite the Name!)
**Status:** ‚úÖ SAFE for tree nut allergies (with rare exceptions)

**Explanation:**
- Coconut is NOT a tree nut - it's a fruit (seed) of a coconut palm tree
- Botanically, it's classified as a drupe (like peaches, plums)
- UK guidance from Allergy UK and Anaphylaxis UK: Generally safe for tree nut allergy sufferers
- No general recommendation to avoid coconut

**Caution:**
- Very rare cases of cross-reactivity have been reported (2 documented cases with walnuts)
- If you have concerns, consult your allergist
- First exposure to coconut should be supervised if you have severe tree nut allergy

**UK Products:**
- Coconut milk, Coconut cream (safe)
- Coconut oil (safe)
- Coconut flour (safe)
- Desiccated coconut (safe)
- Bounty bars (safe for tree nut allergy - check other ingredients)

### Nutmeg - SAFE (Despite the Name!)
**Status:** ‚úÖ SAFE for tree nut allergies

**Explanation:**
- Nutmeg is NOT a tree nut - it's a spice

**Status:** ‚úÖ SAFE for tree nut allergies

- They grow in water, like rice
- Completely unrelated to tree nuts

### Pine Nuts - COMPLICATED (Usually Safe, But...)
**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è USUALLY SAFE, but individual advice needed

**Explanation:**
- Pine nuts are seeds, not tree nuts
- They come from pine cones (Pinus species)
- NOT in the same botanical family as tree nuts
- Cross-reactivity with tree nuts is uncommon

**Recommendation:**
- If you have tree nut allergy, pine nuts are generally safe
- If you think you've had a reaction to pine nuts, see your GP
- Traditional pesto contains pine nuts - check if you're uncertain
- Your allergist can provide individual guidance

**Common Products:**

### "May Contain" Warnings
**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è Assess individual risk

**Explanation:**
- "May contain tree nuts" or "May contain nuts" indicates possible cross-contamination
- March 2025 FSA guidance: Should specify which nuts (e.g., "may contain almonds")
- Only used when unavoidable cross-contact risk exists despite cleaning/segregation

**Recommendation:**
- For severe allergies: Avoid products with "may contain" warnings
- For mild allergies: Discuss with your allergist
- Risk is generally low but not zero

- Nut essences and extracts contain nut proteins (UNSAFE)

**Safe Alternatives:**
- Artificial/synthetic almond flavoring (benzaldehyde) - usually safe, but check
- Vanilla extract (safe)

### Safety Principles for Tree Nuts:

1. **Always check processed foods** - Tree nuts hide in unexpected places (pesto, desserts, sauces)
2. **Don't be fooled by names** - Coconut, nutmeg, water chestnuts are NOT tree nuts
3. **Cross-reactivity matters** - Many people allergic to one tree nut react to others
4. **Peanuts are different** - Peanuts are legumes, not tree nuts (separate allergen)
5. **UK labels are reliable** - Tree nuts must be highlighted in bold on UK labels
6. **"May contain" warnings are important** - Assess risk with allergist for severe allergies
7. **Seeds are safe** - Sunflower, pumpkin, sesame, chia seeds are NOT tree nuts
