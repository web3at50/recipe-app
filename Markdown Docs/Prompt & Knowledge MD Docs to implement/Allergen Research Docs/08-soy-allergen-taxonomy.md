## Background: What is Soy/Soya?

Soy (US spelling) / Soya (UK spelling) refers to soybeans (Glycine max) and products derived from them. Soya is one of the **14 major allergens** that must be declared under UK food law.

**Prevalence:** Soya allergy is most common in infants and young children, though it can occur at any age. Many children outgrow it by age 10.

**Where it hides:** Soya is found in approximately **60% of manufactured foods** in the UK, making it one of the most difficult allergens to avoid.

**UK Terminology:**
- **"Soya"** is the standard UK spelling (used on UK food labels)
- **"Soy"** is the US spelling (but also appears on UK products)
- Both refer to the same allergen

For this app, treat all soy/soya allergies as requiring strict avoidance unless specifically advised otherwise by a healthcare professional.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è TRICKY CASES - Important Notes

### Soya Lecithin (E322) - OFTEN TOLERATED

**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è May be safe for MANY people with soya allergy
**Explanation:** Soya lecithin is a highly refined emulsifier derived from soya beans. Most people with soya allergy can tolerate soya lecithin because it contains **very little soya protein** (the allergenic component).

**UK Guidance:**
- Allergy UK states: "Most people with soya allergy can tolerate soya lecithin"
- NHS guidance: "You may be able to tolerate soya lecithin... ask your dietitian for specific advice"
- Individual tolerance varies - some people react, most don't

**Where Found:**
- Chocolate (very common)
- Baked goods
- Margarine
- Processed foods as emulsifier
- Listed as "E322" or "lecithin (soya)"

**Recommendation for App:**
- Flag as: "Contains soya lecithin (E322). Many people with soya allergy can tolerate this, but individual tolerance varies. Consult with your healthcare provider."
- Allow users to customize if they can tolerate lecithin

---

### Refined Soya Oil - USUALLY SAFE

**Status:** ‚úÖ SAFE for most people with soya allergy
**Explanation:** Fully refined soya oil has the soya protein removed during the refining process. The UK Food Standards Agency advises that refined soya oil should be safe for people with soya allergy.

**UK Regulatory Status:**
- Fully refined soya oil is **exempt from allergen declaration** under UK law
- This means products can contain refined soya oil without declaring "soya" on the label

**Safe:**
- Refined soya oil (fully refined)
- Products containing refined soya oil

**UNSAFE:**
- Cold-pressed soya oil (contains protein - MUST be declared)
- Unrefined soya oil
- Crude soya oil

**Where Found:**
- Vegetable oil blends
- Cooking oils
- Fried foods
- Margarine and spreads
- Salad dressings

**Problem for Users:**
- If a product contains only refined soya oil, it won't say "soya" on the label
- Difficult to track

**Recommendation for App:**
- If user can tolerate refined soya oil: Accept recipes with "vegetable oil" or "refined soya oil"
- If user cannot tolerate ANY soya oil: Flag "vegetable oil" as potentially problematic and suggest specific oils (olive, rapeseed, sunflower)

### Edamame = Soybeans - UNSAFE

**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE - these ARE soybeans!
**Explanation:** Edamame are immature green soybeans, eaten whole in their pods. They are pure soya and highly allergenic.

**Common Confusion:**
- Many people don't realize edamame are soybeans
- UK labeling requires: "edamame (soya)" to make this clear
- Often served in Japanese restaurants as appetizers

**Where Found:**
- Japanese restaurants
- Sushi restaurants
- Asian cuisine
- Frozen vegetable mixes
- Salads
- Snacks

**Recommendation for App:**
- Always flag edamame as containing soya
- Suggest alternatives: garden peas, broad beans, chickpeas

---

### Tamari vs Soy Sauce - BOTH CONTAIN SOYA

**Status:** ‚ùå BOTH UNSAFE for soya allergy
**Explanation:**
- **Soy sauce** = fermented soybeans + wheat + salt
- **Tamari** = fermented soybeans + salt (usually no wheat)
- Tamari is gluten-free but NOT soya-free!

**Common Confusion:**
- People think "tamari" is a safe alternative to soy sauce
- It's only safe for GLUTEN allergies, NOT soya allergies
- Both contain high levels of soya protein

**Recommendation for App:**
- Flag both soy sauce AND tamari as containing soya
- Suggest: Coconut aminos (soy-free, gluten-free alternative)

---

### "Vegetable Oil" - MAY CONTAIN SOYA

**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è DEPENDS on source and refining
**Explanation:** In the UK, "vegetable oil" on a label can be a blend of different oils, potentially including soya oil.

**If fully refined:** Usually safe (see refined soya oil section above)
**If cold-pressed or unrefined:** Would need to declare soya

**Problem:**
- Label just says "vegetable oil" with no specifics
- May be rapeseed, sunflower, soya, or a blend
- If it's refined soya oil, it won't be declared

**Recommendation for App:**
- If user is VERY sensitive or avoids all soya: Flag "vegetable oil" as potentially containing soya, suggest specific oils (olive oil, rapeseed oil, sunflower oil)
- If user can tolerate refined soya oil: Accept "vegetable oil"

### Safety Principles for Soya:

1. **Soya is everywhere** - Found in 60% of UK processed foods, assume processed foods contain soya unless labeled otherwise
2. **Check ALL processed foods** - Soya protein is a cheap filler/extender
3. **Asian cuisine is high-risk** - Japanese, Chinese, Korean, Thai cuisines heavily use soya sauce, tofu, miso
4. **Vegetarian/vegan products almost always contain soya** - It's the primary plant protein
5. **Fresh/natural is usually safe** - Fresh meat, fish, dairy, fruits, vegetables are soya-free
6. **UK labels are reliable** - Soya must be declared (except fully refined soya oil)
7. **Lecithin and refined oil are often tolerated** - But individual tolerance varies
8. **"Vegetable" is a red flag** - Could mean soya (vegetable oil, vegetable protein, vegetable fat)
