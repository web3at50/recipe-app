## Background: What is Fish Allergy?

Fish allergy is one of the **14 major allergens** regulated under UK law (Natasha's Law). It refers to finned fish species (not shellfish, which is a separate allergen category).

**Key Facts:**
- Fish allergy affects approximately 1% of the UK population
- It can cause severe allergic reactions including anaphylaxis
- Unlike many childhood allergies, fish allergy often persists into adulthood
- People allergic to one type of fish are often allergic to others (cross-reactivity)
- **Fish vs Shellfish:** These are DIFFERENT allergen categories with different proteins

**Main Allergenic Protein:**
The primary allergen in fish is **parvalbumin**, a protein found in fish muscle tissue. This protein is:
- Heat-stable (cooking doesn't destroy it)
- Present in all finned fish species
- Can trigger reactions even in small amounts

**Important Distinction:**
- **FISH** = Finned fish (cod, salmon, tuna, etc.) - THIS taxonomy
- **SHELLFISH/CRUSTACEANS** = Prawns, crab, lobster - DIFFERENT allergen
- **MOLLUSCS** = Squid, mussels, oysters - DIFFERENT allergen

For this app, treat fish allergy as requiring strict avoidance of all finned fish and fish-derived products.

### Hidden Sources of Fish (UK-Specific)

**Sauces and Condiments That Often Contain Fish:**
- **Worcestershire sauce** (Lea & Perrins contains anchovies - check for vegan versions)
- **Caesar salad dressing** (most brands contain anchovies)
- Some barbecue sauces (may contain Worcestershire sauce)
- Some pasta sauces (puttanesca often contains anchovies)
- Some salad dressings (especially Caesar, anchovy, or "gourmet" varieties)
- Thai curry pastes (may contain fish sauce or shrimp paste)
- Vietnamese dipping sauces (nuoc cham - contains fish sauce)
- Some pizza toppings (anchovies, or anchovy-based sauces)

**Ready Meals and Convenience Foods:**
- Shepherd's pie (may contain Worcestershire sauce)
- Some gravy mixes (check labels)
- Some stock cubes (fish stock, or regular stock with fish extract)
- Paella (Spanish rice dish - usually contains fish and/or seafood)
- Bouillabaisse (French fish stew)
- Sushi, Sashimi (raw fish)
- Fried rice (may contain fish sauce in seasoning)

**Asian Cuisine - High Risk:**
- Thai dishes (most use fish sauce - pad Thai, green curry, tom yum)
- Vietnamese dishes (most use fish sauce - pho, spring rolls, banh mi)
- Filipino dishes (patis - fish sauce widely used)
- Burmese dishes (ngapi - fermented fish paste)
- Some Chinese dishes (especially southern Chinese cooking)
- Some Japanese dishes (dashi stock may contain bonito/katsuobushi flakes)

**Unexpected Products:**
- Some vitamin D supplements (derived from fish liver oil)
- Some omega-3 supplements (fish oil - unless algae-based)
- Isinglass (fish gelatin used to clarify some beers and wines)
- Some pet foods (can cause contact reactions)
- Some fertilizers (fish meal, fish emulsion)

**Always Check Labels For:**
- Fish sauce, Fish extract
- Anchovy, Anchovy paste
- Fish stock, Fish broth
- Omega-3 (specify source - fish or algae)
- DHA/EPA (specify source - fish or algae)
- Surimi (imitation crab - made from fish)

### Shellfish and Seafood (DIFFERENT Allergen!)

**Important:** Fish and shellfish are DIFFERENT allergen categories

**Crustaceans (Shellfish - Different Allergen):**
- Prawns, Shrimp
- Crab (brown crab, spider crab)
- Lobster
- Crayfish, Langoustine
- **Cross-contamination risk:** These may be processed in the same facilities as fish

**Molluscs (Different Allergen):**
- Mussels, Oysters, Clams
- Squid, Octopus, Cuttlefish
- Whelks, Winkles, Cockles
- Scallops
- **Cross-contamination risk:** These may be processed in the same facilities as fish

**Important Note:**
While shellfish/molluscs are technically DIFFERENT allergens from fish:
- Many people with fish allergy also have shellfish allergy (but not all!)
- Cross-contamination is common in seafood processing and restaurants
- For this app: If user has ONLY fish allergy (not shellfish), shellfish are technically safe BUT warn about cross-contamination risk

## ‚ö†Ô∏è TRICKY CASES - Important Notes

### Worcestershire Sauce - UNSAFE (Contains Anchovies)
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE for fish allergy
**Explanation:** Traditional Worcestershire sauce (Lea & Perrins, most UK brands) contains anchovies as a key ingredient.
**Common in:** Shepherd's pie, cottage pie, stews, marinades, Bloody Mary cocktails, some barbecue sauces
**Safe Alternative:** Henderson's Relish (Sheffield product, fish-free), or vegan Worcestershire sauce
**Warning:** Many processed foods contain Worcestershire sauce - check labels carefully!

---

### Caesar Dressing - UNSAFE (Contains Anchovies)
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE for fish allergy
**Explanation:** Traditional Caesar dressing is made with anchovies (or anchovy paste) as an essential ingredient.
**Common in:** Caesar salads, chicken Caesar wraps, some pasta dishes
**Safe Alternative:** Vegan Caesar dressing (made without anchovies), or make your own with garlic, lemon, mustard, and mayo
**Tip:** Always ask restaurants if their Caesar dressing contains anchovies

---

### Fish Sauce in Asian Cuisine - VERY HIGH RISK
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE - contains concentrated fish
**Explanation:** Fish sauce (nam pla, nuoc mam) is a staple in Thai, Vietnamese, and Filipino cooking. Made from fermented fish, it's used in almost every dish.
**High-risk dishes:**
- Thai: Pad Thai, green curry, tom yum soup, papaya salad
- Vietnamese: Pho, spring rolls, banh mi, nuoc cham dipping sauce
- Filipino: Adobo, sinigang, pancit

**Safe Alternative:** Soy sauce, coconut aminos, mushroom-flavored soy sauce
**Restaurant Warning:** Even vegetable dishes in Thai/Vietnamese restaurants often contain fish sauce - always ask!

---

### Surimi (Imitation Crab) - UNSAFE (Made from Fish)
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE - made from fish (usually pollock)
**Explanation:** Surimi, sold as "imitation crab sticks" or "seafood sticks", is NOT made from crab. It's made from white fish (usually pollock) that's been processed and flavored.
**Common in:** Sushi (California rolls), seafood salads, seafood pasta
**Warning:** The name is misleading - it contains REAL FISH even though it says "imitation crab"

---

### Omega-3 Supplements - DEPENDS ON SOURCE
**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è CHECK SOURCE - fish oil vs algae oil
**Explanation:** Most omega-3 supplements are fish oil-based (unsafe). However, algae-based omega-3 supplements are safe.
**UNSAFE:** Fish oil, cod liver oil, krill oil (contains omega-3 from fish/crustaceans)
**SAFE:** Algae oil, algal oil, "plant-based omega-3", "vegan omega-3"
**Look for labels:** "Derived from algae", "Plant-based", "Suitable for vegans"
**Tip:** Algae oil provides the same DHA/EPA as fish oil without the fish allergen

---

### Isinglass (Fish Gelatin) in Drinks
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE - derived from fish
**Explanation:** Isinglass is a type of gelatin obtained from fish bladders, used to clarify (filter) some beers, wines, and ciders.
**Common in:** Traditional cask ales, some wines, some ciders
**Amount:** Usually trace amounts (filtered out), but may still trigger allergies
**Safe Alternative:** Look for vegan-friendly beers/wines (use plant-based fining agents)
**UK Examples:** Many UK real ales use isinglass - check brewery websites or ask

---

### Fish Stock and Dashi - UNSAFE
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE for fish allergy
**Explanation:** Fish stock is made by simmering fish bones/heads. Dashi (Japanese stock) often uses bonito flakes (dried fish flakes).
**Common in:** Soups, stews, risottos, Japanese cuisine (miso soup, ramen)
**Safe Alternatives:**
- Vegetable stock
- Chicken stock, Beef stock
- Kombu dashi (kelp-only, no bonito)
- Mushroom stock

---

### Taramasalata - UNSAFE (Contains Fish Roe)
**Status:** ‚ùå UNSAFE - contains cod roe (fish eggs)
**Explanation:** Taramasalata is a Greek/Mediterranean dip made from fish roe (usually cod roe or carp roe).
**Common in:** Mezze platters, Greek restaurants, supermarket dip selections
**Safe Alternatives:** Hummus, baba ganoush, tzatziki (yogurt dip)

---

### Fish vs Shellfish - NOT THE SAME ALLERGEN
**Important:** Fish allergy and shellfish allergy are DIFFERENT
**Explanation:**
- **Fish allergy** = Reaction to finned fish (parvalbumin protein)
- **Shellfish allergy** = Reaction to crustaceans/molluscs (tropomyosin protein)
- **Different proteins** = Separate allergies

**Key Points:**
- You can be allergic to fish but not shellfish (and vice versa)
- About 15-20% of people with fish allergy also have shellfish allergy
- If user has ONLY fish allergy ‚Üí Shellfish are technically safe (but warn about cross-contamination)
- If user has BOTH ‚Üí Avoid all seafood

**Cross-Contamination Risk:**
- Shared processing facilities
- Shared fryers in restaurants (fish and chips with scampi)
- Shared cutting boards, utensils
- For severe fish allergy: May want to avoid shellfish restaurants even if not allergic

---

### Vegetarian/Vegan Dishes May Still Contain Fish
**Status:** ‚ö†Ô∏è CHECK CAREFULLY
**Explanation:** "Vegetarian" doesn't always mean fish-free in some cultures
**Examples:**
- Thai vegetarian dishes may still use fish sauce (check!)
- Some vegetable stocks contain fish extract
- Some Asian "vegetable" broths contain fish or shellfish
- Worcestershire sauce in vegetarian dishes (contains anchovies)

**Best Practice:** Always specify "fish allergy" rather than just asking if vegetarian
